# Account Form Field Guide

## What Each Field Means

### 1. Account Name
**What it is**: A nickname or label you give to your account to identify it easily.

**Examples**:
- "My Savings"
- "Emergency Fund"
- "Vacation Money"
- "HDFC Salary Account"
- "Investment Portfolio"

**Why it's useful**: When you have multiple accounts, this helps you quickly identify which one is which. It's like a custom label just for you.

**Placeholder**: "e.g., My Savings, Emergency Fund"

---

### 2. Type
**What it is**: The category or purpose of your account.

**Options**:
- **Checking**: Your everyday spending account (like a salary account)
- **Savings**: Money you're saving for the future
- **Credit**: Credit card accounts (tracks what you owe)
- **Investment**: Stocks, mutual funds, or other investments
- **Cash**: Physical money you hold (auto-created)

**Why it's useful**: Helps organize your accounts by purpose and shows different colors for each type.

---

### 3. Initial Balance
**What it is**: How much money is currently in this account when you're adding it to the app.

**Example**: If your bank account has ₹50,000 right now, enter 50000

**Note**: 
- Only shown when creating a new account
- After creation, use "Adjust Balance" button to add/remove money
- This is just the starting point

---

### 4. Currency
**What it is**: Which currency your account uses.

**Options**:
- **INR (₹)**: Indian Rupees
- **USD ($)**: US Dollars

**Why it's useful**: Shows the correct currency symbol (₹ or $) on your account cards and throughout the app.

---

### 5. Bank Selection (Optional)
**What it is**: Which bank or financial institution this account is with.

**Examples**:
- HDFC Bank
- State Bank of India (SBI)
- ICICI Bank
- Axis Bank

**Why it's useful**: 
- Shows the bank logo on your account card
- Helps you quickly identify which bank each account belongs to
- Completely optional - you can skip this if you want

**How to use**: Just start typing the bank name (e.g., "HDFC") and select from the dropdown.

---

## Example: Creating a Savings Account

Let's say you want to add your HDFC savings account:

```
Account Name: "HDFC Savings"
Type: Savings
Initial Balance: 75000
Currency: INR (₹)
Bank Selection: HDFC Bank
```

This will create a card that shows:
- Name: "HDFC Savings"
- Type badge: "SAVINGS" (in green)
- Balance: ₹75,000
- HDFC Bank logo

---

## Example: Creating an Emergency Fund

For a separate emergency fund:

```
Account Name: "Emergency Fund"
Type: Savings
Initial Balance: 100000
Currency: INR (₹)
Bank Selection: (leave empty or select a bank)
```

---

## Special Account: Cash

The "Cash" account is automatically created and represents physical money you hold (like cash in your wallet or at home).

**Restrictions**:
- Cannot change the name
- Cannot change the type
- Cannot change the currency
- Cannot delete it
- Can only adjust the balance using "Adjust Balance" button

---

## Tips

1. **Use descriptive names**: Instead of "Account 1", use "Salary Account" or "Emergency Savings"

2. **Be accurate with balance**: Enter the current balance as shown in your bank statement

3. **Bank selection is optional**: If you don't want to show bank logos, you can skip this field

4. **You can edit later**: Except for the Cash account, you can edit account details anytime (but not the balance - use "Adjust Balance" for that)

5. **Multiple accounts**: You can create as many accounts as you want to track all your money in one place
